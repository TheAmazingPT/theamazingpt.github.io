Installing FreeBSD 11 on Thinkpad T420

Rationale

All my young life i was a Windows guy, mainly because of the games i was playing on the computer. This stopped with Windows XP, eventhough I experienced some Windows 7. I dived into the world of GNU/Linux and its various distributions. My favourite one was Debian, preferably i would the netinstall image to construct my ideal working environment. Arch Linux was a solid alternative for my regular reinstall orgies. At some point I started to work for a company, which uses only Apple devices. There was a backend developer, who had the freedom to use Linux on his Dell notebook, which was my backdoor opportunity to install a FreeBSD 10 system on a Macbook Air. Maybe i will provide a write up on this topic in the future.

Anyway, i have no idea why i had to bore you with my history of operating systems. At some point i just fell in love with FreeBSD, because of its organisation, philosophy and history. I love the purity and design of this system and I feel like I could learn and master this system at some point. And I also love ThinkPad's. Both form the perfect and most comfortable working environment for me. I spend my leisure time in this system too, of course.

Outline

I am going to show the simple installation process of FreeBSD 11, how to setup networking and how to install desktop with i3. I am using i3 as a dm, Firefox as my browser and rxvt-unicode with tmux as my working tools.
